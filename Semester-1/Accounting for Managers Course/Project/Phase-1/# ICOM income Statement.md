#### 1. ICON Group and Its Operations

##### 1.1 General information

- The Industrial Engineering Company for Construction and Development "ICON" (S.A.E) was established under Presidential Decree No. 169 of 1977 in accordance with the provisions of Law No. 26 of 1954, which was published in the Official Gazette on 28 April 1977. - The Managing Director is Mr. Ahmed Enaba.

#### 3. Significant events during the period

**Projects under construction**
The increase is primarily due to new investments in Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development – KSA (Tatweer), which recorded additions related to two new production lines for light steel structures currently being installed. The increase also includes additions in Acrow Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork related to expansions in administrative buildings and projects to enhance the plant's electrical capacity. Industrial Engineering Company for Construction and Development (ICON Egypt) recorded an increase due to site preparation works for leveling and improving the Al-Goura land. ICON Prefabricated Buildings and Caravans also saw an increase as construction continued on the welding workshop project. Conversely, ICON Panel recorded a decrease following the capitalization of the new warehouse after it became ready for use.

**Investments in Associates**
The main reason for the increase is the Group's share in the profits of Acrow DZ – Algeria and the impact of foreign currency translation.

**Inventory**
Inventory decreased during the period as a result of management's strategic actions and market conditions. The reduction primarily reflects a shift to a lean inventory model, sourcing raw materials based on confirmed customer orders rather than maintaining excess stock, and aligning production with a produce-to-order approach. In addition, management temporarily halted raw material purchases amid stable exchange rates and the absence of indicators for currency devaluation, opting to utilize existing inventory for ongoing projects. Changes in tonnage costs and material mix, coupled with revised purchasing policies at certain subsidiaries adopting quarterly procurement cycles, further contributed to the decline. Timing differences in material deliveries.

**Trade and notes receivable**
The decrease is primarily due to the parent company's acquisition of Acrow Gulf, which was reclassified as a related party. The comparative balance as of 31 December 2024, amounting to EGP 645.99 million, was previously presented under trade receivables; this reclassification resulted in a reduction in trade receivables and a corresponding increase in related-party balances. Additionally, Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development - KSA (Tatweer) recorded a sharp decline in receivables, driven by collections of prior-year balances and a substantial drop in current-period revenue.

**Due from related parties**
The increase was primarily driven by the acquisition of Acrow Gulf by AIF Holding Limited (the parent company), which resulted in its classification as a related party effective during the year, was previously presented under trade receivables. This movement was partially offset by increase in the Expected Credit Loss provision.

**Debtors and other debit balances**
Other receivables recorded a notable increase at Acrow Misr due to higher Value Added Taxes balances resulting from increased purchases and export sales, as well as higher advance tax payments following the adoption of the advance payment system with the Egyptian Tax Authority. Additional increases were driven by advances to suppliers for operational purchases and accrued revenues. Conversely, ICON Panels recorded a significant decrease due to settlement of supplier advances, lower Value Added Taxes balances, and reduced prepaid expenses. Industrial Engineering Company for Construction and Development (ICON Egypt) also recorded a decrease following the utilization of withholding tax balances to settle tax obligations and the clearance of advances related to excavation works. Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development - KSA (Tatweer) recorded a decrease after the release of guarantee amounts upon project completion.

**Deferred tax liabilities**
The decrease is mainly due to a reduction in deferred tax liabilities related to unrealized foreign exchange gains.

**Trade and notes payable**
The increase is primarily driven by procurement activities to support higher production and sales volumes, particularly at Acrow Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork. The increase reflects significant purchases from both foreign and local suppliers, including major steel and material vendors.

**Creditors and other credit balances**
The increase was primarily driven by Acrow Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork, which recorded substantial growth due to higher advances from clients, accrued expenses, and tax-related liabilities. This movement was complemented by increases in governmental creditor and social insurance accruals and accrued fright costs due to higher export sales. In contrast, ICON Panel, ICON Prefabricated Buildings and Caravans, and Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development - KSA (Tatweer) reported significant decreases in advances from customers as prior-period balances were utilized through invoicing and revenue recognition, consistent with project execution progress.

**Current income tax liabilities**
The decrease is primarily due to advance payments made during the period and the settlement of the outstanding balance for the year ended 2024. Additionally, income tax expense recognized during the period declined as a result of lower income before tax.

**Revenue from contracts with customers**
The increase is primarily driven by strong performance at Acrow Group, supported by ACROW Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork and its subsidiaries such as Morocco, Oman, and AICS, which benefited from major infrastructure and industrial projects and new customer acquisitions in addition to the higher export sales and the appreciation of foreign currencies against the Egyptian pound. ICON Panel contributed significantly through increased domestic demand for sandwich panels, reflecting expansion among local industrial and construction clients. ICON Prefabricated Projects also recorded notable growth, driven by new contracts with leading companies including Orascom Construction, El Masria for Engineering and Construction, and other major contractors. Conversely, Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development - KSA (Tatweer) experienced a sharp decline due to the completion of prior-year high-value contracts and delays in new projects, while Industrial Engineering Company for Construction and Development (ICON Egypt) saw reduced operating revenue following the strategic spin-off of project activities.

**Cost of revenue**
The increase is primarily driven by strong performance at Acrow Group, supported by ACROW Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork as recorded a significant increase in cost of sales, primarily driven by higher raw material consumption, expanded production volumes, and inflationary pressures following the EGP devaluation, alongside increased labor and logistics costs. ICON Panel and ICON Prefabricated Buildings and Caravans also experienced notable cost increases, largely attributable to elevated raw material prices and higher operating expenses linked to production growth and macroeconomic factors. In contrast, Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development - KSA (Tatweer) reported a substantial decline in cost of sales, consistent with the sharp reduction in revenue following the completion of major projects in the prior year and the absence of comparable contracts in the current period. Industrial Engineering Company for Construction and Development (ICON Egypt) recorded a significant decrease due to the spin-off of operational activities to related entities, resulting in the transfer of material and labor costs to subsidiaries.

**Expected credit losses on Financial Assets**
The decrease in expected credit loss provisions as of 30 September 2025, compared to 30 September 2024, is primarily due to a notable improvement in macroeconomic factors used in the measurement model. The current period witnessed a decline in inflation rates and relative stability in the local currency exchange rate, along with positive cash collections from customers, which positively impacted credit risk assessment. It is worth noting that the economic factors used in the prior period's model were more conservative due to high inflation and exchange rate volatility, resulting in a lower provision in the current period compared to the previous one.

**General and Administrative Expenses**
ACROW Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork recorded the most significant increase, primarily driven by higher employee-related costs, annual salary adjustments, short-term incentive accruals, and professional fees. ICON Panel and ICON Prefabricated Buildings and Caravans also reported notable increases, largely attributable to wage adjustments, relocation allowances, and higher consulting and audit fees, alongside incremental administrative costs such as office expenses and depreciation of IT and office equipment. ICON Separate experienced a moderate increase, influenced by salary adjustments, and higher utilities and audit fees, partially offset by reductions in lease expenses and consultancy costs following the group spin-off. Industrial Prefabricated Buildings Development - KSA (Tatweer) increase was mainly due to depreciation on newly capitalized buildings, combined with higher salaries and service contract costs, offset by reductions in marketing and professional fees.

**Selling and Marketing Expenses**
The increase was primarily driven by ACROW Misr for Scaffolding & Formwork, The largest contributor was employee-related costs, which rose significantly due to expanded marketing activities, additional headcount, and annual salary adjustments, compounded by foreign currency exposure on branch payrolls. Other notable increases were linked to marketing initiatives, including participation in international exhibitions, which involved substantial costs for event participation, promotional materials, and sample products.

**Finance income**
The decrease in foreign exchange gains as of 30 September 2025, is due to the relative stability of the Egyptian pound exchange rate during period, which limited the realization of foreign exchange gains compared to the previous period. It is worth noting that the Central Bank of Egypt announced on 6 March 2024, the liberalization of foreign currency exchange rates against the Egyptian pound according to market mechanisms, which led to an increase in the USD exchange rate in local banks from EGP 30 to EGP 48 during Q1 2024, resulting in significant foreign exchange gains during that period. In the current period, the relative stability of the exchange rate reduced such gains.

**Finance costs**
The increase in finance costs during the period was mainly due to higher interest expenses resulting from increased borrowings and bank facilities, as the group relied more heavily on these facilities during the period to cover operational and financing needs.

**Income Tax**
The decrease is primarily attributable to the reduction in deferred tax expenses related to unrealized foreign exchange gains, as well as a decline in income tax expense recognized during the period, driven by lower income before tax.

#### 15. Liquidity risks

Liquidity risk is the risk that the Group will not be able to meet its financial obligations as they fall due, as a result of shortage of funding. Group's exposure to liquidity risk results primarily from the mismatching of maturities of financial assets and liabilities.

The management makes cash flow projections on periodic basis, and takes the necessary actions to negotiate with suppliers, follow-up the collections and manage the inventory balances in order to ensure sufficient cash is maintained to discharge the Group's liabilities. The Group's management monitors liquidity for operation reserves by ensuring it has sufficient cash and cash equivalents to meet operational needs while maintaining sufficient cash cover to meet the cash outflows to settle the liabilities of loans and borrowings to be able to maintain financial terms, guarantees and covenants at all times.

The Group limits liquidity risks by maintaining sufficient bank facilities and reserves, and by monitoring future and actual cash flows and matching the maturity portfolio of financial assets and liabilities. Balances due to suppliers are normally settled within an average of 60 days from the date of purchase.

#### 16. Significant events during the reporting period

##### (a)

During the reporting period, the Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) of the Central Bank of Egypt implemented several key interest rate adjustments in response to evolving economic conditions. On 20 February 2025, the MPC maintained policy rates at historically high levels (overnight deposit at 27.25%, lending at 28.25%, and main operation at 27.75%). Subsequently, the Committee initiated a series of rate cuts: 225 basis points on 17 April, 100 basis points on 22 May, and maintained rates on 10 July. Further reductions followed, with a 200 basis point cut on 28 August and a 100 basis point cut on 2 October, bringing the overnight deposit rate down to 21.00% by October. On 20 November 2025, the MPC held rates steady at these levels. These decisions reflect the Committee's ongoing assessment of inflation dynamics and economic outlook throughout the year.

##### (b)

On 13 June 2025, an armed conflict erupted in the Middle East, raising concerns about its potential impact on the Egyptian economy. Following intensive negotiations, the parties involved reached a ceasefire agreement on 24 June 2025. The Group is currently assessing these developments carefully to understand their overall impact on its performance and future strategies.

##### (c)

During the reporting period, Acrow Misr for Metallic Scaffolding and Formwork initiated the process for voluntary delisting from the Egyptian Exchange (EGX). Following the approval of the Financial Regulatory Authority and in compliance with Article 55 of the Listing Rules and Article 228 of the Executive Regulations of Law No. 159 of 1981, the company has scheduled an Extraordinary General Assembly on 6 January 2026 to approve the voluntary delisting of its shares. The agenda includes endorsing the fair value study prepared by an independent financial advisor and approving the purchase of shares from affected shareholders at the highest price among the fair value, the highest closing price in the month preceding the board's decision, or the average closing price over the prior three months. Additionally, a temporary account will be established to execute the buyback transactions, and the Chairman will be authorized to complete all necessary procedures with regulatory authorities.
